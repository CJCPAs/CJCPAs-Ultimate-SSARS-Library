# AR-C Section 80: Compilation Engagements

## Overview

AR-C Section 80 provides guidance for compilation engagements. A compilation engagement involves presenting in the form of financial statements, information that is the representation of management without undertaking to express any assurance on the statements. Unlike a preparation engagement, a compilation requires the accountant to issue a report.

---

## üìú Official AICPA Language

### Objective

> "The objective of the accountant in a compilation engagement is to apply accounting and financial reporting expertise to assist management in the presentation of financial statements and report in accordance with this section without undertaking to obtain or provide any assurance that there are no material modifications that should be made to the financial statements in order for the statements to be in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework." (AR-C 80.02)

### Scope

> "This section applies when an accountant is engaged to perform a compilation engagement." (AR-C 80.01)

### Definition

> "Compilation engagement‚ÄîAn engagement to apply accounting and financial reporting expertise to assist management in the presentation of financial statements without obtaining or providing any assurance that there are no material modifications that should be made to the financial statements in order for the statements to be in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework." (AR-C 80.05)

---

## üìù Plain English Interpretation

**What AR-C 80 Means for You:**

A compilation is a step up from a preparation engagement. You're still not verifying the information, but you are:
- Applying your accounting expertise
- Assisting management in presenting financial statements
- Issuing a formal report
- Stating no assurance is provided

**Think of It This Way:**
- **Preparation** = Format the information, add legend, no report
- **Compilation** = Same formatting + professional report stating no assurance

**Key Distinction:** A compilation report accompanies the financial statements.

---

## üéØ Independence Requirements

### Official Language

> "When performing compilation engagements, the accountant is not required to be independent of the entity for which the accountant is reporting." (AR-C 80.14)

> "When the accountant is not independent, the accountant's compilation report should specifically disclose that the accountant is not independent... The accountant should not include the reason for the lack of independence in this disclosure." (AR-C 80.21)

### Plain English

**Independence is NOT required for compilation engagements, BUT:**

1. If you are NOT independent, you must disclose this fact in your report
2. You cannot disclose WHY you're not independent
3. You may optionally disclose the reason, but this is not required or recommended

### Independence Disclosure Example

If not independent, add this paragraph to your report:

```
I am (We are) not independent with respect to XYZ Company.
```

**That's it!** Do not add reasons like:
- ‚ùå "...because I have a financial interest in the company"
- ‚ùå "...because my spouse is an employee"
- ‚ùå "...because I provide bookkeeping services"

## üìã Engagement Requirements

### 1. Engagement Letter (Required)

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should agree upon the terms of the engagement with management or those charged with governance, as appropriate... [T]he agreed-upon terms of the engagement should be documented in an engagement letter or other suitable written agreement." (AR-C 80.07)

**Required Contents:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Objective and scope** | Compile financial statements per specified framework |
| **Management responsibilities** | Preparation and fair presentation of financial statements |
| **Accountant responsibilities** | Apply accounting expertise, issue compilation report |
| **Limitations** | No assurance provided |
| **Financial reporting framework** | GAAP, OCBOA, or other specified framework |
| **Expected form/content of report** | Compilation report to be issued |

### 2. Understanding the Entity

**Official Language:**
> "Prior to the issuance of the compilation report, the accountant should obtain an understanding of the entity sufficient to enable the accountant to apply accounting and financial reporting expertise to assist management in the presentation of financial statements." (AR-C 80.10)

**What This Means:**
- Understand the client's industry
- Know the entity's operations
- Understand significant accounting policies
- Be familiar with the applicable financial reporting framework

### 3. Reading the Financial Statements

**Official Language:**
> "Prior to the issuance of the compilation report, the accountant should read the financial statements and consider whether such financial statements appear to be appropriate in form and free from obvious material errors." (AR-C 80.11)

**Plain English:**
You should:
- Read through the financial statements
- Look for obvious errors
- Consider whether format is appropriate
- NOT perform verification procedures

### 4. Management's Knowledge and Responsibilities

**Official Language:**
> "If the accountant becomes aware that the financial statements do not adequately refer to or describe the applicable financial reporting framework, the accountant should inform management." (AR-C 80.12)

Management is responsible for:
- The content of the financial statements
- Selecting the appropriate financial reporting framework
- The design, implementation, and maintenance of internal control

## üìÑ Compilation Reports

### Standard Compilation Report

**Official Language Provides Standard Report Format:**

```
Management is responsible for the accompanying financial statements of 
XYZ Company, which comprise the balance sheet as of December 31, 20XX, 
and the related statements of income, changes in stockholders' equity, 
and cash flows for the year then ended, and the related notes to the 
financial statements in accordance with accounting principles generally 
accepted in the United States of America. I (We) have performed a 
compilation engagement in accordance with Statements on Standards for 
Accounting and Review Services promulgated by the Accounting and Review 
Services Committee of the AICPA. I (We) did not audit or review the 
financial statements nor was (were) I (we) required to perform any 
procedures to verify the accuracy or completeness of the information 
provided by management. I (We) do not express an opinion, a conclusion, 
nor provide any form of assurance on these financial statements.

[Signature of accounting firm or accountant, as appropriate]

[Accountant's city and state]

[Date of the accountant's report]
```

### Compilation Report - Not Independent

When not independent, add this sentence after the standard report language:

```
I am (We are) not independent with respect to XYZ Company.
```

### Compilation Report - Substantially All Disclosures Omitted

When disclosures are omitted, add:

```
Management has elected to omit substantially all the disclosures 
(and the statement of cash flows) required by accounting principles 
generally accepted in the United States of America. If the omitted 
disclosures were included in the financial statements, they might 
influence the user's conclusions about the company's financial 
position, results of operations, and cash flows. Accordingly, the 
financial statements are not designed for those who are not informed 
about such matters.
```

### Compilation Report - Known Departure from Framework

When there is a known departure from the applicable financial reporting framework:

```
Management has elected to omit substantially all the disclosures 
required by accounting principles generally accepted in the United 
States of America. If the omitted disclosures were included in the 
financial statements, they might influence the user's conclusions 
about the company's financial position, results of operations, and 
cash flows. Accordingly, the financial statements are not designed 
for those who are not informed about such matters.

Additionally, as disclosed in Note X to the financial statements, 
the company has not capitalized the cost of certain leasehold 
improvements, which is a departure from accounting principles 
generally accepted in the United States of America. The effects of 
this departure on the financial statements have not been determined.
```

## üìä Disclosures in Compilation Engagements

### With Full Disclosures

Financial statements include all required disclosures:
- All notes required by the framework
- Complete statement of cash flows
- Most comprehensive presentation

### With Substantially All Disclosures Omitted

**Official Language:**
> "If management elects to omit substantially all disclosures required by the applicable financial reporting framework, the accountant should modify the compilation report to clearly indicate that substantially all disclosures required by the applicable financial reporting framework have been omitted." (AR-C 80.18)

**Requirements:**
1. Must disclose the omission in the report
2. State that if included, disclosures might influence user's conclusions
3. May also omit statement of cash flows
4. Common for internal use or when users are informed about the entity

### Disclosures That Aren't Substantially All

If only SOME disclosures are omitted:
- This is a departure from the framework
- Must be disclosed in report (see departure section)
- Cannot use "substantially all omitted" language

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Departures from the Financial Reporting Framework

### Known Departures

**Official Language:**
> "If the accountant becomes aware that the financial statements are materially misstated, the accountant should propose appropriate revisions to management. If management does not make the revisions, the accountant should consider whether modification of the standard report is adequate to disclose the departure." (AR-C 80.19)

**Process:**
1. Become aware of departure
2. Propose corrections to management
3. If management refuses:
   - Modify report to disclose departure
   - OR withdraw from engagement if appropriate
4. Document the departure and your response

### Reporting on Departures

Include in your report:
- Description of the departure
- Effects on financial statements (if known)
- If effects not determined, state so

## üìã Documentation Requirements

### What to Document

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should prepare documentation in connection with each compilation engagement that provides a sufficient record to document the engagement, including the procedures performed." (AR-C 80.24)

**Required Documentation:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Engagement letter** | Signed agreement with client |
| **Financial statements** | Copy of compiled financial statements |
| **Compilation report** | Copy of report issued |
| **Understanding obtained** | Evidence of understanding the entity |
| **Reading procedures** | Evidence of reading the financial statements |
| **Significant matters** | Any issues, departures, communications |
| **Independence documentation** | Assessment of independence |

### Documentation Timing

- Complete within 60 days of report date (recommended)
- Cannot add documentation after assembly
- Retain per firm policy and regulations

## ‚úÖ Compilation Engagement Checklist

### Pre-Engagement

- [ ] Evaluate client acceptance/continuance
- [ ] Assess competence to perform engagement
- [ ] Determine independence status
- [ ] Understand financial reporting framework
- [ ] Prepare and send engagement letter
- [ ] Obtain signed engagement letter

### During Engagement

- [ ] Obtain understanding of the entity
- [ ] Obtain understanding of significant accounting policies
- [ ] Obtain financial information from management
- [ ] Apply accounting and financial reporting expertise
- [ ] Read the financial statements
- [ ] Consider if statements appear appropriate and free from obvious errors
- [ ] Identify any departures from framework
- [ ] Communicate significant matters to management

### Reporting

- [ ] Determine appropriate report language
- [ ] Include required modifications (if any):
  - [ ] Not independent disclosure
  - [ ] Substantially all disclosures omitted
  - [ ] Known departures
- [ ] Date the report
- [ ] Sign the report

### Post-Engagement

- [ ] Issue financial statements with compilation report
- [ ] Document the engagement
- [ ] Assemble workpapers
- [ ] Invoice client

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Peer Review Deficiencies

### Report Issues

1. **Incorrect report language** - Use current standard language
2. **Missing independence disclosure** - Required when not independent
3. **Incomplete departure disclosure** - Must describe departure adequately
4. **Dating errors** - Report date must be appropriate

### Documentation Issues

1. **No engagement letter** - Required for all compilations
2. **No evidence of understanding** - Document understanding of entity
3. **No evidence of reading** - Document reading the financial statements
4. **Missing independence assessment** - Document independence evaluation

### Technical Issues

1. **Incorrect disclosure omission language** - Use exact required wording
2. **Departures not properly modified** - All known departures must be disclosed
3. **Inappropriate financial statement format** - Follow framework requirements

## üìñ Practical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Full Disclosure Compilation - Independent

**Situation:** Compilation of annual financial statements with all disclosures, accountant is independent.

**Report:** Standard compilation report, no modifications needed.

### Scenario 2: Substantially All Disclosures Omitted - Independent

**Situation:** Annual compilation, client wants statements for internal use without notes.

**Report:** Standard report plus disclosure omission paragraph.

### Scenario 3: Full Disclosure Compilation - Not Independent

**Situation:** You provide bookkeeping services to the client, now compiling year-end statements.

**Report:** Standard report plus independence disclosure. Do NOT state why you're not independent.

### Scenario 4: Known Departure from GAAP

**Situation:** Client uses LIFO for inventory but doesn't maintain adequate records to support LIFO.

**Action:**
1. Propose correction to management
2. If refused, modify report to describe departure
3. State effects if determinable

### Scenario 5: Management Refuses to Sign Engagement Letter

**Situation:** Client wants compilation but won't sign engagement letter.

**Action:** Cannot proceed with compilation engagement. Engagement letter is required by professional standards.

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I compile financial statements for a client I'm not independent of?

Yes, but you must disclose the lack of independence in your report. You cannot disclose the reason for the lack of independence.

### What's the difference between preparation and compilation?

The main differences are:
- Compilation requires a report; preparation requires a legend
- Compilation report may need independence disclosure; preparation has no such requirement
- Third parties generally prefer compilations over preparations

### Can I omit the statement of cash flows?

Yes, when substantially all disclosures are omitted, you may also omit the statement of cash flows. This must be indicated in your report.

### What if management won't correct a material misstatement?

Modify your report to disclose the departure, or consider withdrawing from the engagement if the misstatement is sufficiently significant.

### Must I obtain a management representation letter?

A management representation letter is recommended but not required for compilation engagements. It is required for review engagements.
