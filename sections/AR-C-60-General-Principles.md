# AR-C Section 60: General Principles for Engagements Performed in Accordance With SSARS

## Overview

AR-C Section 60 establishes the general principles applicable to all engagements performed under Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services (SSARS). This section serves as the foundation for understanding the accountant's responsibilities across all SSARS services.

---

## üìú Official AICPA Language

### Objective

> "The objective of the accountant is to apply the requirements relevant to the engagement in accordance with this section." (AR-C 60.02)

### Scope

> "This section applies to engagements performed in accordance with SSARS. When performing an engagement in accordance with SSARS, the accountant should have an understanding of the entire text of a section, including its application and other explanatory material, to understand its objectives and apply its requirements properly." (AR-C 60.01)

---

## üìù Plain English Interpretation

**What AR-C 60 Means for You:**

AR-C 60 is the "umbrella" section that covers fundamental concepts applicable to ALL SSARS engagements. Think of it as the rules that apply regardless of whether you're preparing, compiling, or reviewing financial statements.

**Key Takeaways:**
1. You must understand the entire SSARS framework, not just the section for your specific engagement
2. Professional judgment is essential throughout every engagement
3. Ethical requirements, including integrity and objectivity, always apply
4. Quality management standards apply to SSARS engagements
5. You must document your work sufficiently

---

## üéØ Key Requirements

### 1. Ethical Requirements

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should comply with relevant ethical requirements, including those pertaining to independence, when applicable." (AR-C 60.06)

**Plain English:**
Every SSARS engagement requires you to follow the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct. This includes:
- Acting with integrity
- Being objective
- Using professional competence and due care
- Maintaining confidentiality
- Following independence rules (when applicable)

### 2. Professional Judgment

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should exercise professional judgment in planning and performing an engagement performed in accordance with SSARS." (AR-C 60.07)

**Plain English:**
You cannot just mechanically follow a checklist. Every situation requires thoughtful consideration of:
- The specific circumstances of the engagement
- The nature of the entity
- The needs of the intended users
- Your own knowledge and experience

### 3. Conduct of the Engagement

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should:
> a. plan the engagement so that it will be performed effectively;
> b. perform the engagement with professional competence; and
> c. comply with the requirements of the applicable section that are relevant to the engagement." (AR-C 60.08)

**Plain English:**
1. **Plan First** - Know what you're doing before you start
2. **Be Competent** - Have the knowledge and skills needed
3. **Follow the Rules** - Use the appropriate AR-C section requirements

### 4. Documentation Requirements

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should prepare documentation sufficient to enable an experienced accountant, having no previous connection with the engagement, to understand the nature, timing, and extent of the procedures performed to comply with SSARS and applicable legal and regulatory requirements." (AR-C 60.12)

**Plain English:**
Your documentation should be complete enough that another CPA could:
- Pick up your files
- Understand what you did
- Understand why you did it
- Understand your conclusions

## üìã Documentation Standards

### What Must Be Documented

| Element | Description | Requirement Level |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Engagement letter | Agreement with client | Required |
| Procedures performed | Nature, timing, extent | Required |
| Significant findings | Material issues identified | Required |
| Conclusions reached | Professional judgments made | Required |
| Report issued | Final deliverable | Required |

### Documentation Timing

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should assemble the documentation in an engagement file and complete the administrative process of assembling the final engagement file on a timely basis after the date of the report." (AR-C 60.13)

**Plain English:**
- Complete your documentation promptly after issuing the report
- AICPA recommends 60-day assembly deadline
- Once assembled, don't delete or discard documentation before retention period ends

### Retention Requirements

Per QM Standards and firm policies, typically:
- Minimum 5 years from report date
- State board rules may vary
- Consider client agreements
- Consider insurance requirements

## ‚úÖ Compliance Checklist

### Before Accepting an Engagement

- [ ] Evaluate ethical requirements, including integrity of management
- [ ] Assess your competence to perform the engagement
- [ ] Confirm you can comply with all applicable requirements
- [ ] Consider independence requirements for the service type
- [ ] Establish understanding with client (engagement letter)

### During the Engagement

- [ ] Apply professional judgment throughout
- [ ] Maintain objectivity
- [ ] Follow applicable AR-C section requirements
- [ ] Document procedures and conclusions
- [ ] Consult when appropriate

### After the Engagement

- [ ] Assemble documentation timely
- [ ] Issue appropriate report
- [ ] Complete administrative matters
- [ ] Maintain confidentiality
- [ ] Retain documentation per policy

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Mechanical Approach**
   - Don't just follow checklists without thinking
   - Apply professional judgment to each situation

2. **Inadequate Documentation**
   - Document your reasoning, not just procedures
   - Another accountant should understand your work

3. **Ignoring Ethical Requirements**
   - Ethics apply to every engagement
   - Independence varies by service type

4. **Insufficient Planning**
   - Plan before performing
   - Understand the entity and engagement

5. **Failure to Stay Current**
   - SSARS are periodically updated
   - Monitor for new pronouncements

## üìù Practical Application

### Scenario: New Client Acceptance

**Situation:** A potential new client approaches you to prepare their financial statements. How does AR-C 60 apply?

**Application of AR-C 60:**
1. **Ethical Evaluation** - Assess the integrity of the client's management
2. **Competence Check** - Confirm you have industry knowledge
3. **Understanding Required** - Prepare engagement letter
4. **Plan the Engagement** - Determine timing and procedures
5. **Documentation** - Set up workpapers for the engagement

### Scenario: Discovering Errors

**Situation:** During a compilation, you discover material errors in the client's books.

**Application of AR-C 60:**
1. **Professional Judgment** - Determine significance of errors
2. **Communicate** - Discuss findings with management
3. **Document** - Record your findings and conclusions
4. **Consider Impact** - How does this affect the engagement?
5. **Ethical Requirements** - Maintain integrity in your response
