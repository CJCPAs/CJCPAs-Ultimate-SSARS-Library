# AR-C Section 120: Compilation of Pro Forma Financial Information

## Overview

AR-C Section 120 provides guidance for engagements to compile pro forma financial information. Pro forma financial information shows what the financial statements "would have looked like" if a particular transaction or event had occurred at an earlier date. This section is specialized and applies specifically to pro forma presentations.

### Objective

> "The objective of the accountant when performing a compilation engagement for pro forma financial information is to assist management in presenting the pro forma financial information in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework and report in accordance with this section without undertaking to obtain or provide any assurance that there are no material modifications that should be made to the pro forma financial information in order for it to be in accordance with the applicable criteria." (AR-C 120.03)

### Scope

> "This section applies when an accountant is engaged to compile pro forma financial information." (AR-C 120.01)

### Definition

> "Pro forma financial informationâ€”Financial information that shows the significant effects on historical financial information as if a consummated or proposed transaction (or event) had occurred at an earlier date." (AR-C 120.05)

## ğŸ“ Plain English Interpretation

**What is Pro Forma Financial Information?**

Pro forma financials answer the question: "What would our financial statements have looked like IF..."

**Common Uses:**
- Business combination (merger or acquisition)
- Disposal of a significant business portion
- Change in capitalization (new debt, equity)
- Change in form of business organization
- Proposed transaction being evaluated

**Key Characteristics:**
- Based on historical financial information
- Adjusted for a specific transaction or event
- Shows "as if" the transaction occurred earlier
- Different from prospective financial statements (forecasts/projections)

**Example:**
> Company A acquires Company B on October 1, 20XX. Pro forma financial statements show what the combined company's results would have been if the acquisition had occurred on January 1, 20XX.

## ğŸ¯ Requirements for Pro Forma Compilations

### 1. Underlying Financial Statements

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant may compile pro forma financial information only when the accountant has compiled, reviewed, or audited the historical financial statements on which the pro forma financial information is based." (AR-C 120.09)

**Requirements:**

| Historical F/S Level | Pro Forma Compilation Permitted? |
|---------------------|----------------------------------|
| Audited | Yes |
| Reviewed | Yes |
| Compiled | Yes |
| Prepared (AR-C 70) | No |
| Not issued by accountant | No |

### 2. Engagement Letter (Required)

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should agree upon the terms of the engagement with management or those charged with governance, as appropriate, and document such agreed-upon terms in an engagement letter or other suitable written agreement." (AR-C 120.06)

**Required Contents:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Objective and scope** | Compile pro forma financial information |
| **Management responsibilities** | Assumptions, adjustments, presentation |
| **Accountant responsibilities** | Compile per SSARS, issue report |
| **Limitations** | No assurance provided |
| **Transaction or event** | Specific transaction addressed |
| **Basis of pro forma** | Historical statements used |

### 3. Understanding the Transaction

**Official Language:**
> "Prior to the issuance of the report, the accountant should obtain an understanding of the transaction or event, the underlying historical financial statements, and the presentation of the pro forma financial information." (AR-C 120.10)

**What to Understand:**

- Nature of the transaction or event
- Relevant dates (occurrence, pro forma date)
- How adjustments were determined
- Assumptions made by management
- Applicable financial reporting framework

### 4. Reading and Evaluating Pro Forma Information

**Official Language:**
> "Prior to the issuance of the report, the accountant should read the pro forma financial information, including the summary of significant assumptions, and consider whether such pro forma financial information appears to be appropriate in form and free from obvious material errors." (AR-C 120.11)

**Considerations:**

- [ ] Pro forma adjustments appear reasonable
- [ ] Assumptions are clearly disclosed
- [ ] Presentation is appropriate
- [ ] No obvious material errors
- [ ] Framework requirements followed

## ğŸ“„ Pro Forma Compilation Report

### Standard Pro Forma Compilation Report

**Official Language Provides Report Format:**

```
Independent Accountant's Compilation Report

[Appropriate Addressee]

Management is responsible for the accompanying pro forma financial
information of XYZ Company as of December 31, 20XX, and for the year
then ended. The pro forma adjustments are based on management's
assumptions as described in Note X. I (We) have performed a compilation
engagement in accordance with Statements on Standards for Accounting
and Review Services promulgated by the Accounting and Review Services
Committee of the AICPA. I (We) did not audit or review the pro forma
financial information nor was (were) I (we) required to perform any
procedures to verify the accuracy or completeness of the information
provided by management. Accordingly, I (we) do not express an opinion,
a conclusion, nor provide any form of assurance on the pro forma
financial information.

The objective of pro forma financial information is to show what the
significant effects on the historical financial information might have
been had the [describe the transaction or event] occurred at an earlier
date. However, the pro forma financial information is not necessarily
indicative of the results of operations or related effects on financial
position that would have been attained had the above-mentioned
[transaction or event] actually occurred at an earlier date.

[Signature of accounting firm or accountant, as appropriate]

[Accountant's city and state]

[Date of the accountant's report]
```

### Modified Report Elements

If the pro forma financial information contains a known departure from the criteria used to compile pro forma financial information, modify the report to describe the departure.

## ğŸ“‹ Pro Forma Adjustments

### Types of Pro Forma Adjustments

| Adjustment Type | Description | Example |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| **Transaction adjustments** | Directly related to the transaction | Purchase price allocation |
| **Reclassification adjustments** | Conform accounting presentation | Different account classifications |
| **Financing adjustments** | Related to financing the transaction | Interest on acquisition debt |
| **Tax adjustments** | Tax effects of adjustments | Tax on eliminated intercompany profits |

### Adjustment Documentation

For each adjustment, document:
- Nature and purpose
- How amount was determined
- Source of information
- Assumptions involved
- Expected to have continuing impact?

### Assumptions in Pro Forma Adjustments

**Management's Assumptions Should Be:**
- Clearly stated
- Reasonable
- Consistent with each other
- Consistent with historical information
- Supportable by evidence

## ğŸ¯ Independence Requirements

### Official Language

> "When performing a compilation engagement for pro forma financial information, the accountant is not required to be independent of the entity." (AR-C 120.14)

### Plain English

**Independence is NOT required for pro forma compilations.**

However:
- If not independent, must disclose in report
- Same rules as regular compilation (AR-C 80)
- Cannot disclose reason for lack of independence

### Independence Disclosure

If not independent, add to report:

```
I am (We are) not independent with respect to XYZ Company.
```

## âš ï¸ Common Pitfalls and Errors

### Presentation Issues

1. **Unclear assumptions** - All assumptions must be clearly disclosed
2. **Incomplete adjustments** - All significant adjustments should be included
3. **Missing explanatory notes** - Notes should explain adjustments
4. **Inappropriate dating** - Pro forma date should be clear

### Adjustment Issues

1. **Unsupported amounts** - Adjustments should be supportable
2. **Mathematical errors** - Verify accuracy of calculations
3. **Inconsistent assumptions** - Assumptions should not conflict
4. **Tax effects ignored** - Consider tax implications of adjustments

### Documentation Issues

1. **No engagement letter** - Required for all engagements
2. **No evidence of reading** - Document reading for obvious errors
3. **Missing historical F/S** - Must have appropriate historical statements
4. **Insufficient understanding** - Document understanding of transaction

## ğŸ“– Practical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Business Acquisition

**Situation:** Company A acquires Company B on December 1, 20XX. Management wants pro forma financial statements showing combined operations for the full year.

**Pro Forma Approach:**
1. Start with Company A's historical financial statements (must be compiled, reviewed, or audited)
2. Add Company B's operations for entire year
3. Eliminate intercompany transactions
4. Allocate purchase price
5. Show combined operations as if acquisition occurred January 1
6. Compile the pro forma information
7. Issue pro forma compilation report

### Scenario 2: Disposition of Segment

**Situation:** Company sells a significant division on October 1, 20XX. Wants pro forma showing operations without the division.

**Pro Forma Approach:**
1. Start with historical financial statements
2. Remove division's results for entire period
3. Adjust for any shared costs that will continue
4. Adjust for gain/loss on disposition
5. Show operations as if disposition occurred earlier
6. Compile the pro forma information
7. Issue pro forma compilation report

### Scenario 3: New Debt Financing

**Situation:** Company issues significant new debt. Wants pro forma showing effects on financial position and interest expense.

**Pro Forma Approach:**
1. Start with historical financial statements
2. Adjust balance sheet for new debt
3. Adjust for use of proceeds (acquisitions, debt repayment, etc.)
4. Calculate pro forma interest expense
5. Consider tax effects
6. Compile the pro forma information
7. Issue pro forma compilation report

## ğŸ”„ Comparison: Pro Forma vs. Prospective Financial Information

| Feature | Pro Forma | Prospective (Forecasts/Projections) |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------------------|
| **Base** | Historical financial statements | Future expectations |
| **Purpose** | Show effect of past/proposed transaction | Predict future results |
| **Time reference** | "As if" earlier date | Future periods |
| **SSARS section** | AR-C 120 | Not covered by SSARS |
| **Adjustments** | Transaction-specific | Based on assumptions about future |

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I compile pro forma if I only prepared the historical statements?

No. AR-C 120 requires that you have compiled, reviewed, or audited the historical financial statements. Preparation engagements (AR-C 70) do not qualify.

### Can another CPA compile the pro forma if we audited the historical statements?

The standard requires that the accountant compiling the pro forma financial information be the one who compiled, reviewed, or audited the historical statements. However, the other CPA could use the audited statements as a basis if appropriate arrangements are made.

### What is the difference between pro forma and forecasts?

Pro forma shows effects of a transaction on historical information ("what would have been"). Forecasts predict future results ("what will be"). Different standards apply.

### Must assumptions be disclosed?

Yes. All significant assumptions used in developing pro forma adjustments must be clearly disclosed in the notes or summary of assumptions.

### Can I include pro forma with regular compiled financial statements?

Yes, but each requires its own report language. The pro forma compilation report is specific to pro forma information.

### What if the historical statements are more than one year old?

Consider whether the historical statements are still relevant for the pro forma presentation. Stale historical data may not provide meaningful pro forma information.

2. **Pro forma shows "as if" effects** - Not a forecast of future

4. **Assumptions must be disclosed** - Clear and complete disclosure
