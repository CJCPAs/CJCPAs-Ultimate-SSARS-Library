# AR-C Section 90: Review of Financial Statements

## Overview

AR-C Section 90 provides guidance for review engagements. A review engagement provides **limited assurance** on financial statements, which is more than a compilation but less than an audit. The accountant performs analytical procedures and makes inquiries to obtain a reasonable basis for expressing limited assurance that there are no material modifications that should be made to the financial statements.

**This is the highest level of service available under SSARS.**

---

## üìú Official AICPA Language

### Objective

> "The objective of the accountant when performing a review of financial statements is to obtain limited assurance as a basis for reporting whether the accountant is aware of any material modifications that should be made to the financial statements for them to be in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework, primarily through the performance of inquiry and analytical procedures." (AR-C 90.03)

### Scope

> "This section applies when an accountant is engaged to perform a review of financial statements." (AR-C 90.01)

### Definition

> "Review engagement‚ÄîAn engagement to perform review procedures on the financial statements and report on them. The report expresses limited assurance, which is less than reasonable assurance." (AR-C 90.07)

> "Limited assurance‚ÄîA level of assurance that is less than the reasonable assurance obtained in an audit engagement but is at an acceptable level as the basis for the conclusion expressed in the accountant's review report." (AR-C 90.07)

## üéØ Independence Requirements

### Official Language

> "The accountant should be independent when performing a review engagement." (AR-C 90.18)

> "If the accountant is not independent, the accountant should not report on the financial statements in accordance with this section." (AR-C 90.19)

### Plain English

**Independence is MANDATORY for review engagements.**

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Independence maintained | Proceed with review |
| Independence impaired | Cannot issue review report |
| Independence impaired | May step down to compilation (AR-C 80) |
| Independence impaired | Must consider if compilation appropriate |

### Independence Threats

Common threats to independence in SSARS engagements:

| Threat | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Self-interest** | Financial interest in client, loans, fee dependence |
| **Self-review** | Preparing records you then review |
| **Advocacy** | Promoting client's position |
| **Familiarity** | Close relationships with client personnel |
| **Undue influence** | Threats or pressure from client |

### Safeguards

If threats exist, apply safeguards:
- Separate personnel for bookkeeping vs. review
- Independent partner review
- External consultation
- Documentation of threat evaluation

**Bottom Line:** If you cannot maintain independence, you CANNOT perform a review.

## üìã Engagement Requirements

### 1. Engagement Letter (Required)

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should agree upon the terms of the engagement with management or those charged with governance, as appropriate... The agreed-upon terms of the engagement should be documented in an engagement letter or other suitable written agreement." (AR-C 90.10)

**Required Contents:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Objective and scope** | Review financial statements per specified framework |
| **Management responsibilities** | Preparation and fair presentation; internal control; provide information |
| **Accountant responsibilities** | Conduct review per SSARS; issue report expressing limited assurance |
| **Limitations** | Not an audit; no opinion expressed |
| **Financial reporting framework** | GAAP or other specified framework |
| **Expected form of report** | Review report with limited assurance conclusion |
| **Management representation letter** | Will be required from management |

### 2. Understanding the Entity and Its Environment

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should obtain an understanding of the entity and its environment, including the entity's internal control, and the applicable financial reporting framework and the entity's accounting policies and practices sufficient to identify areas in the financial statements where there is a greater likelihood that material misstatements may arise and thereby provide a basis for designing procedures to address those areas." (AR-C 90.24)

**What to Understand:**

| Category | Specifics |
|----------|-----------|
| **Industry** | Nature of business, economic conditions, regulatory environment |
| **Entity** | Ownership, governance, organizational structure |
| **Accounting** | Significant accounting policies, estimates, judgments |
| **Internal control** | General understanding (not testing) |
| **Framework** | GAAP or other basis, recent changes |

### 3. Analytical Procedures (Required)

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should apply analytical procedures to the financial statements. The analytical procedures should be designed to identify relationships and individual items that appear to be unusual and that may indicate a material misstatement." (AR-C 90.25)

**Types of Analytical Procedures:**

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Trend analysis** | Compare current to prior periods | Revenue increased 50% - why? |
| **Ratio analysis** | Compare financial ratios | Current ratio, debt-to-equity |
| **Reasonableness tests** | Assess if amounts make sense | Interest expense vs. debt level |
| **Industry comparison** | Compare to industry benchmarks | Gross margin vs. industry average |
| **Budget vs. actual** | Compare to expectations | Actual vs. budgeted amounts |

**Documentation of Analytical Procedures:**
- Procedures performed
- Expectations developed
- Results obtained
- Conclusions reached
- Follow-up on unexpected results

### 4. Inquiries of Management and Others (Required)

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should make the following inquiries of management:
> a. Whether the financial statements have been prepared and presented in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework
> b. Whether there have been any changes in the entity's business activities
> c. Matters about which questions have arisen during the performance of the review
> d. Events subsequent to the date of the financial statements that could have a material effect on the financial statements
> e. Knowledge of any fraud or suspected fraud affecting the entity
> f. Known noncompliance with laws and regulations
> g. Related-party transactions
> h. Litigation, claims, and assessments
> i. Whether management has disclosed all known subsequent events" (AR-C 90.26)

**Inquiry Areas Checklist:**

- [ ] Financial statement preparation
- [ ] Business changes
- [ ] Accounting policy changes
- [ ] Related party transactions
- [ ] Subsequent events
- [ ] Fraud or suspected fraud
- [ ] Litigation, claims, assessments
- [ ] Going concern considerations
- [ ] Noncompliance with laws
- [ ] Significant estimates
- [ ] Commitments and contingencies

### 5. Management Representation Letter (Required)

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should request a written representation from management for all financial statements and periods covered by the accountant's review report." (AR-C 90.32)

**Required Representations:**

| Representation | Purpose |
|---------------|---------|
| Responsibility for financial statements | Confirms management's duty |
| Fair presentation | Confirms no material misstatements |
| Completeness of information | All info provided to accountant |
| All related parties disclosed | Complete disclosure |
| All subsequent events considered | Nothing omitted |
| All fraud known to management | No concealment |
| No uncorrected material misstatements | Amounts are correct |

**If Management Refuses to Sign:**
- Cannot issue review report
- Consider withdrawing from engagement
- Document the situation
- Consider implications for relationship

## üìÑ Review Reports

### Standard Review Report

**Official Language Provides Standard Report Format:**

```
Independent Accountant's Review Report

[Appropriate Addressee]

I (We) have reviewed the accompanying financial statements of XYZ Company,
which comprise the balance sheet as of December 31, 20XX, and the related
statements of income, changes in stockholders' equity, and cash flows for
the year then ended, and the related notes to the financial statements.

Management's Responsibility for the Financial Statements

Management is responsible for the preparation and fair presentation of
these financial statements in accordance with accounting principles
generally accepted in the United States of America; this includes the
design, implementation, and maintenance of internal control relevant to
the preparation and fair presentation of financial statements that are
free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error.

Accountant's Responsibility

My (Our) responsibility is to conduct the review engagement in accordance
with Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services promulgated
by the Accounting and Review Services Committee of the AICPA. Those
standards require me (us) to perform procedures to obtain limited assurance
as a basis for reporting whether I am (we are) aware of any material
modifications that should be made to the financial statements for them to
be in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted in the
United States of America. I (We) believe that the results of my (our)
procedures provide a reasonable basis for my (our) conclusion.

I (We) am (are) required to be independent of XYZ Company and to meet my
(our) other ethical responsibilities, in accordance with the relevant
ethical requirements related to my (our) review.

Accountant's Conclusion

Based on my (our) review, I am (we are) not aware of any material
modifications that should be made to the accompanying financial statements
in order for them to be in accordance with accounting principles generally
accepted in the United States of America.

[Signature of accounting firm or accountant, as appropriate]

[Accountant's city and state]

[Date of the accountant's report]
```

### Modified Review Report - Known Departure from Framework

When there is a known material departure:

```
[Standard paragraphs through Accountant's Conclusion]

Basis for Qualified Conclusion

As disclosed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company has not
capitalized lease obligations that should be capitalized in accordance
with accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of
America. If these lease obligations were capitalized, property and
equipment would be increased by $XXX, long-term debt would be increased
by $XXX, and net income (loss) would be (increased/decreased) by $XXX.

Qualified Conclusion

Based on my (our) review, except for the matter described in the Basis
for Qualified Conclusion paragraph, I am (we are) not aware of any
material modifications that should be made to the accompanying financial
statements in order for them to be in accordance with accounting
principles generally accepted in the United States of America.
```

### Modified Review Report - Scope Limitation

When there is a scope limitation:

```
[Standard paragraphs]

Basis for Qualified Conclusion

I (We) were unable to obtain sufficient appropriate review evidence about
[describe the limitation]. [Describe the possible effects.]

Qualified Conclusion

Based on my (our) review, except for the possible effects of the matter
described in the Basis for Qualified Conclusion paragraph, I am (we are)
not aware of any material modifications that should be made to the
accompanying financial statements in order for them to be in accordance
with accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of
America.
```

### Adverse Conclusion

When departures are so significant that a qualified conclusion is not adequate:

```
[Standard paragraphs through Accountant's Responsibility]

Basis for Adverse Conclusion

As discussed in Note X, the Company has not consolidated the financial
statements of XYZ subsidiary. This subsidiary was acquired on [date] and
its financial statements reflect total assets and revenues constituting
XX% and XX%, respectively, of the consolidated totals. The Company's
investment in the subsidiary is recorded on the cost basis.

Adverse Conclusion

Based on my (our) review, due to the significance of the matter described
in the Basis for Adverse Conclusion paragraph, the financial statements
are not in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted in
the United States of America.
```

### Key Financial Statement Ratios

#### Liquidity Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | Short-term debt-paying ability |
| Quick Ratio | (Cash + Receivables) / Current Liabilities | Immediate liquidity |
| Cash Ratio | Cash / Current Liabilities | Most conservative liquidity |

#### Profitability Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Gross Profit Margin | Gross Profit / Revenue | Markup on products/services |
| Net Profit Margin | Net Income / Revenue | Overall profitability |
| Return on Assets | Net Income / Total Assets | Asset efficiency |
| Return on Equity | Net Income / Equity | Shareholder return |

#### Activity Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| A/R Turnover | Revenue / Avg. Receivables | Collection efficiency |
| Inventory Turnover | COGS / Avg. Inventory | Inventory efficiency |
| A/P Turnover | COGS / Avg. Payables | Payment patterns |
| Days Sales Outstanding | 365 / A/R Turnover | Average collection period |

#### Leverage Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Debt / Total Equity | Capital structure |
| Debt-to-Assets | Total Debt / Total Assets | Leverage level |
| Interest Coverage | EBIT / Interest Expense | Ability to cover interest |
| Times Interest Earned | EBIT / Interest Expense | Debt service ability |

### Analytical Procedures by Financial Statement Area

#### Revenue
- Compare to prior periods
- Analyze by product line/segment
- Consider economic conditions
- Compare to industry trends
- Review monthly patterns

#### Expenses
- Compare percentage to revenue
- Compare to prior periods
- Analyze fixed vs. variable
- Consider known changes
- Review for unusual items

#### Assets
- Verify reasonableness of balances
- Compare to prior periods
- Review for obsolescence
- Consider collectibility
- Analyze turnover rates

#### Liabilities
- Verify completeness
- Compare to prior periods
- Review for unrecorded items
- Analyze debt covenants
- Consider subsequent payments

## üìã Documentation Requirements

### What to Document

**Official Language:**
> "The accountant should prepare documentation that is sufficient to enable an experienced accountant, having no previous connection with the engagement, to understand the work performed, the significant judgments made, and the basis for the accountant's conclusion." (AR-C 90.37)

**Required Documentation:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Engagement letter** | Signed agreement with client |
| **Understanding of entity** | Knowledge obtained about the entity |
| **Analytical procedures** | Procedures performed, expectations, results |
| **Inquiries** | Questions asked and responses received |
| **Management representation letter** | Signed representation letter |
| **Significant matters** | Issues identified, how resolved |
| **Communication** | Significant communications with management |
| **Review report** | Copy of report issued |
| **Independence assessment** | Documentation of independence evaluation |

### Documentation Best Practices

1. **Contemporaneous** - Document as you go
2. **Complete** - Include all significant matters
3. **Clear** - Another accountant could understand
4. **Conclusive** - Show your conclusions
5. **Connected** - Link evidence to conclusions

### Documentation Timing

- Complete within 60 days of report date (recommended)
- Assemble documentation file promptly
- Do not add or delete after assembly deadline
- Retain per firm policy (minimum 5 years typical)

- [ ] Make inquiries of management
- [ ] Address inquiry responses
- [ ] Follow up on subsequent events
- [ ] Consider going concern
- [ ] Consider related parties
- [ ] Consider fraud risks

- [ ] Prepare management representation letter
- [ ] Include all required representations
- [ ] Date representation letter same as review report
- [ ] Obtain signed representation letter
- [ ] Evaluate completeness of representations

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Peer Review Deficiencies

### Independence Issues

1. **No documentation of independence assessment** - Must evaluate and document
2. **Performing review when not independent** - Cannot issue review report
3. **Failure to identify threats** - Evaluate threats and safeguards

### Analytical Procedures Issues

1. **Insufficient analytical procedures** - Must perform for all material areas
2. **No expectations documented** - Must develop and document expectations
3. **No follow-up on differences** - Must investigate unexpected results
4. **Generic procedures** - Must tailor to the entity

### Inquiry Issues

1. **Inquiries not documented** - Must document questions and answers
2. **Insufficient inquiries** - Must cover all required areas
3. **No follow-up on responses** - Must address management responses

### Representation Letter Issues

1. **Missing representation letter** - Required for all reviews
2. **Incomplete representations** - Must include all required matters
3. **Dating errors** - Must match report date
4. **Not signed** - Must have management signature

### Report Issues

1. **Incorrect report language** - Must use current standard language
2. **Dating errors** - Must be dated appropriately
3. **Missing modifications** - Must disclose known departures
4. **Independence statement omitted** - Required in report

### Documentation Issues

1. **Insufficient documentation** - Another accountant must understand
2. **No evidence of understanding** - Must document understanding obtained
3. **Missing workpapers** - Must retain adequate documentation
4. **Late assembly** - Complete within 60 days

## üìñ Practical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Standard Review - Independent

**Situation:** Annual review of financial statements with full disclosures. You are independent and no modifications needed.

**Report:** Standard review report with unmodified conclusion.

**Key Steps:**
1. Engagement letter
2. Obtain understanding
3. Analytical procedures
4. Inquiries
5. Representation letter
6. Standard report

### Scenario 2: Known Departure from GAAP

**Situation:** Client uses cash basis for GAAP statements but calls them GAAP.

**Action:**
1. Propose correction to management
2. If management refuses, determine materiality
3. Issue qualified conclusion disclosing departure
4. Describe departure and effects in report

### Scenario 3: Management Refuses to Sign Representation Letter

**Situation:** Management will not sign the required representation letter.

**Action:**
1. Cannot issue review report
2. Consider whether compilation is appropriate
3. Discuss implications with management
4. Document the situation
5. Withdraw if appropriate

### Scenario 4: Subsequent Event Discovery

**Situation:** After completing fieldwork but before issuing report, you learn of a material subsequent event.

**Action:**
1. Perform additional procedures on the event
2. Make additional inquiries
3. Consider need for disclosure
4. Evaluate impact on conclusion
5. Update representation letter if needed

### Scenario 5: Going Concern Question

**Situation:** During the review, you notice indicators that the entity may not continue as a going concern.

**Action:**
1. Inquire of management about plans
2. Evaluate management's plans
3. Consider adequacy of disclosure
4. If conditions exist:
   - Ensure adequate disclosure
   - Consider emphasis paragraph
5. Document your evaluation

### Scenario 6: Independence Impairment During Engagement

**Situation:** Midway through the review, you discover your firm provided a service that impairs independence.

**Action:**
1. Stop review procedures immediately
2. Evaluate the independence impairment
3. Cannot issue review report
4. Consider stepping down to compilation
5. Discuss with client
6. Document the situation thoroughly

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between limited and reasonable assurance?

**Limited assurance (review):** Based primarily on inquiries and analytical procedures. Expressed negatively ("I am not aware of any material modifications").

**Reasonable assurance (audit):** Based on extensive testing and evidence gathering. Expressed positively ("In our opinion, the financial statements present fairly").

### Can I step down from a review to a compilation?

Yes, if circumstances warrant (such as independence impairment). However, you must:
- Obtain new engagement letter
- Follow compilation requirements
- Issue compilation report
- Document the change

### What if I discover fraud during a review?

1. Communicate to appropriate level of management
2. If management is involved, communicate to those charged with governance
3. Consider whether to continue the engagement
4. Consider legal and regulatory reporting requirements
5. Document thoroughly
6. Consider impact on conclusion

### How is the review report different from a compilation report?

- Review report expresses a conclusion (limited assurance)
- Compilation report states no assurance is provided
- Review report includes independence statement
- Review report addresses management's and accountant's responsibilities more extensively

### Can substantially all disclosures be omitted in a review?

No, unlike compilations, reviews require full disclosures. Omitting substantially all disclosures is not permitted for review engagements.

### What if the client won't provide information I need?

This is a scope limitation. You must:
1. Attempt to obtain the information
2. Evaluate the effect on your conclusion
3. If material, modify your report
4. If pervasive, consider withdrawal

### Must I obtain a new representation letter each year?

Yes, you must obtain a signed representation letter for each review engagement. It should be dated the same date as your review report.

## üìù Key Takeaways

1. **Independence is mandatory** - Cannot perform a review without independence
2. **Limited assurance is real assurance** - You are expressing a conclusion
3. **Analytical procedures are required** - Not optional like in compilations
4. **Inquiries are required** - Must ask management specific questions
5. **Representation letter is required** - Cannot issue report without it
6. **Documentation must be thorough** - Support your conclusion
7. **Report uses negative assurance** - "Not aware of material modifications"
8. **Third parties rely on reviews** - Take this responsibility seriously
