# Management Representation Letter - Compilation Engagement (AR-C 80)

## Optional Compilation Representation Letter

**NOTE:** A management representation letter is **NOT REQUIRED** for compilation engagements under AR-C 80. However, many practitioners find it useful to obtain written representations from management, particularly in situations involving:

- New clients
- Complex transactions
- Higher-risk engagements
- Client history of adjustments or issues

### Representations

We confirm, to the best of our knowledge and belief, as of **[Date of letter]**:

### Responsibility for Financial Statements

1. We are responsible for the preparation and fair presentation of the financial statements in accordance with **[accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of America / the income tax basis of accounting / the cash basis of accounting / other applicable framework]**.

2. We are responsible for designing, implementing, and maintaining internal control relevant to the preparation and fair presentation of financial statements that are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error.

3. We are responsible for preventing and detecting fraud.

### Completeness of Information

4. We have made available to you all financial records and related data.

5. All transactions have been recorded and are reflected in the financial statements.

6. We have provided you with complete information on all related party relationships and transactions.

### Accuracy of Information

7. The financial statements are free of material misstatements, including omissions.

8. We have disclosed to you all known actual or possible litigation and claims whose effects should be considered when preparing the financial statements.

9. We have no knowledge of any fraud or suspected fraud affecting the entity involving management or employees.

10. We have no knowledge of any allegations of fraud or suspected fraud affecting the entity received in communications from employees, former employees, or others.

### Compliance

11. The entity has complied with all aspects of contractual agreements that may have a material effect on the financial statements.

12. We have no knowledge of any instances of noncompliance with laws and regulations.

### Disclosures (if substantially all disclosures are omitted)

13. **[If applicable:]** We understand that the financial statements omit substantially all disclosures required by **[applicable framework]** and that if the omitted disclosures were included, they might influence the user's conclusions about the company's financial position, results of operations, and cash flows.

### Subsequent Events

14. No events have occurred subsequent to the balance sheet date and through the date of this letter that would require adjustment to or disclosure in the financial statements.

## When to Consider Using This Letter

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| New client | Strongly recommend |
| Complex transactions | Strongly recommend |
| Material adjusting entries | Recommend |
| Prior issues or deficiencies | Recommend |
| High-risk industry | Recommend |
| Established client, simple entity | Optional |

## Key Differences from Review Representation Letter

| Element | Compilation | Review |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Required by standards | No | Yes |
| Dating requirements | Flexible | Must match report date |
| Scope of representations | May be abbreviated | Comprehensive required |
| Consequences if refused | Consider implications | Cannot issue report |
