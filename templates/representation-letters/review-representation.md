# Management Representation Letter - Review Engagement (AR-C 90)

## Standard Review Representation Letter

**IMPORTANT:** This letter is REQUIRED for all review engagements. The accountant cannot issue a review report without obtaining a signed management representation letter.

---

**[CLIENT LETTERHEAD]**

[Date of Accountant's Review Report]

[CPA Firm Name]
[Address]
[City, State ZIP]

This representation letter is provided in connection with your review of the financial statements of **[Company Name]**, which comprise the [balance sheet/statement of financial position] as of **[Date]**, and the related statements of [income/operations], [changes in stockholders' equity/changes in members' capital], and cash flows for the [year/period] then ended, and the related notes to the financial statements, for the purpose of expressing limited assurance that there are no material modifications that should be made to the financial statements in order for them to be in accordance with **[accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of America / other applicable framework]**.

---

### General Representations

We confirm that, to the best of our knowledge and belief, having made such inquiries as we considered necessary for the purpose of appropriately informing ourselves as of **[Date of representation letter]**:

### Financial Statements

1. We have fulfilled our responsibilities, as set out in the terms of the engagement letter dated **[Date]**, for:
   - The preparation and fair presentation of the financial statements in accordance with **[applicable framework]**
   - The design, implementation, and maintenance of internal control relevant to the preparation and fair presentation of financial statements that are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error

2. We acknowledge our responsibility to prevent and detect fraud.

3. We acknowledge our responsibility to identify and ensure that the entity complies with the laws and regulations applicable to its activities.

4. We have provided you with all relevant information and access, as agreed in the terms of the engagement.

5. All transactions have been recorded and are reflected in the financial statements.

6. Significant assumptions used by us in making accounting estimates, including those measured at fair value, are reasonable.

### Information Provided

7. We have provided you with:
   - Access to all information of which we are aware that is relevant to the preparation and fair presentation of the financial statements, such as records, documentation, and other matters
   - Additional information that you have requested from us for the purpose of the review
   - Unrestricted access to persons within the entity from whom you determined it necessary to obtain review evidence

8. We have disclosed to you all known actual or possible litigation and claims whose effects should be considered when preparing the financial statements.

### Fraud and Noncompliance

9. We have disclosed to you the results of our assessment of the risk that the financial statements may be materially misstated as a result of fraud.

10. We have disclosed to you all information in relation to fraud or suspected fraud that we are aware of and that affects the entity and involves:
    - Management
    - Employees who have significant roles in internal control
    - Others where the fraud could have a material effect on the financial statements

11. We have disclosed to you all information in relation to allegations of fraud, or suspected fraud, affecting the entity's financial statements communicated by employees, former employees, analysts, regulators, or others.

12. We have disclosed to you all known instances of noncompliance or suspected noncompliance with laws and regulations whose effects should be considered when preparing financial statements.

### Related Parties

13. We have disclosed to you the identity of the entity's related parties and all related party relationships and transactions of which we are aware.

14. All related party transactions have been appropriately accounted for and disclosed in accordance with the requirements of **[applicable framework]**.

### Subsequent Events

15. We have disclosed to you all events subsequent to the date of the financial statements that require adjustment or disclosure.

16. All events occurring subsequent to the date of the financial statements and for which **[applicable framework]** requires adjustment or disclosure have been adjusted or disclosed.

### Going Concern (if applicable)

17. **[If going concern issues exist:]** We have disclosed to you all information relevant to the use of the going concern assumption, including significant conditions and events, our plans, and the feasibility of those plans.

18. **[If no going concern issues:]** We believe the entity has the ability to continue as a going concern for a reasonable period of time.

### Other Representations

19. We have no knowledge of any side agreements or other arrangements (either written or oral) that have not been disclosed to you.

20. The financial statements disclose all significant estimates, including those measured at fair value, and management's judgments and assumptions related to those estimates.

21. We have appropriately disclosed to you all representations about the entity made to third parties (such as banks, creditors, regulators) that may affect the financial statements.

22. **[Include any additional representations specific to the entity or industry.]**

## Notes for Use

### Dating Requirements

The representation letter must be dated **the same date as the review report**. This ensures that representations cover the entire period up to the report date.

### Signature Requirements

The representation letter should be signed by:
- **Chief Executive Officer** (or equivalent)
- **Chief Financial Officer** (or equivalent)
- Or other members of management with appropriate responsibilities

### If Management Refuses to Sign

If management refuses to provide written representations:
1. You **cannot** issue a review report
2. Consider the effect on your relationship with the client
3. Consider whether to withdraw from the engagement
4. Document the refusal

### Modifications

You may need to add representations for:
- Specific industry matters
- Complex transactions
- Going concern issues
- Unusual circumstances
- Matters identified during the review

### Do Not Omit Required Representations

All representations listed above are required elements. Do not omit any unless they are not applicable to the entity.
