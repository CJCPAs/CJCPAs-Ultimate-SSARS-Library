# Review Report - Emphasis of Matter (AR-C 90)

## Unmodified Conclusion with Emphasis Paragraph

Use this report when:
- The accountant wants to draw users' attention to a matter presented or disclosed in the financial statements
- The matter does NOT result in a modification to the conclusion
- The matter is of fundamental importance to users' understanding of the financial statements

---

## Report Template

```
                    INDEPENDENT ACCOUNTANT'S REVIEW REPORT


To the [Board of Directors / Shareholders / Partners / Members] of
[Company Name]
[City, State]


I (We) have reviewed the accompanying financial statements of [Company Name], which
comprise the [balance sheet / statement of financial position] as of [Month Day,
Year], and the related statements of income, [changes in stockholders' equity /
changes in partners' capital / changes in members' equity], and cash flows for the
[year / period] then ended, and the related notes to the financial statements.


Management's Responsibility for the Financial Statements

Management is responsible for the preparation and fair presentation of these
financial statements in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted
in the United States of America; this includes the design, implementation, and
maintenance of internal control relevant to the preparation and fair presentation
of financial statements that are free from material misstatement, whether due to
fraud or error.


Accountant's Responsibility

My (Our) responsibility is to conduct the review engagement in accordance with
Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services promulgated by the
Accounting and Review Services Committee of the AICPA. Those standards require
me (us) to perform procedures to obtain limited assurance as a basis for
reporting whether I am (we are) aware of any material modifications that should
be made to the financial statements for them to be in accordance with accounting
principles generally accepted in the United States of America. I (We) believe
that the results of my (our) procedures provide a reasonable basis for my (our)
conclusion.

I am (We are) required to be independent of [Company Name] and to meet my (our)
other ethical responsibilities, in accordance with the relevant ethical
requirements related to my (our) review.


Accountant's Conclusion

Based on my (our) review, I am (we are) not aware of any material modifications
that should be made to the accompanying financial statements in order for them
to be in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted in the United
States of America.


Emphasis of Matter

[Choose appropriate example or customize:]

[Going Concern:]
As discussed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company has suffered
recurring losses from operations and has a net capital deficiency that raise
substantial doubt about its ability to continue as a going concern. Management's
plans in regard to these matters are also described in Note X. The financial
statements do not include any adjustments that might result from the outcome
of this uncertainty. My (Our) conclusion is not modified with respect to this
matter.

[Significant Transaction:]
As discussed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company was acquired
by [Acquirer Name] on [Date]. My (Our) conclusion is not modified with respect
to this matter.

[Related Party Transaction:]
As disclosed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company has significant
transactions with related parties. My (Our) conclusion is not modified with
respect to this matter.

[Change in Accounting Principle:]
As discussed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company changed its
method of accounting for [describe] in [Year] due to the adoption of [Standard].
My (Our) conclusion is not modified with respect to this matter.

[Uncertainty:]
As discussed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company is a defendant
in a lawsuit alleging [describe]. The ultimate outcome of this matter cannot
presently be determined. My (Our) conclusion is not modified with respect to
this matter.


[Signature of accounting firm or accountant]

[City, State]

[Date of report]
```

---

## Key Elements

### Emphasis of Matter Paragraph Must:

1. **Have a clear heading** - "Emphasis of Matter"
2. **Reference the note disclosure** - "As discussed in Note X..."
3. **Describe the matter** - Brief explanation
4. **State conclusion is not modified** - "My (Our) conclusion is not modified with respect to this matter."

### Placement

The Emphasis of Matter paragraph should be placed **after** the Accountant's Conclusion paragraph.

---

## Common Situations for Emphasis of Matter

| Situation | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Going concern** | Substantial doubt about ability to continue |
| **Significant subsequent event** | Major event after balance sheet date |
| **Major acquisition or merger** | Change in entity structure |
| **Significant related party transactions** | Material related party activity |
| **Change in accounting principle** | Adoption of new standard |
| **Significant uncertainty** | Major litigation, contingency |
| **Catastrophic event** | Natural disaster, pandemic impact |

---

## Going Concern Example (Expanded)

When there is substantial doubt about going concern:

```
Emphasis of Matter - Going Concern

As discussed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company has experienced
significant operating losses, negative cash flows from operations, and has a
working capital deficiency. These conditions raise substantial doubt about the
Company's ability to continue as a going concern. Management's evaluation of
these events and conditions and management's plans to mitigate these matters
are described in Note X. The financial statements do not include any
adjustments that might result from the outcome of this uncertainty. My (Our)
conclusion is not modified with respect to this matter.
```

---

## Emphasis of Matter vs. Qualified Conclusion

| Emphasis of Matter | Qualified Conclusion |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| No departure from framework | Departure from framework |
| Properly presented/disclosed | Improperly presented |
| Draws attention to matter | Modifies conclusion |
| Conclusion NOT modified | Conclusion IS modified |

---

## Multiple Emphasis Paragraphs

You may include multiple Emphasis of Matter paragraphs if there are several matters warranting emphasis. Each should:
- Have its own paragraph
- Reference specific note
- Include the "not modified" statement
