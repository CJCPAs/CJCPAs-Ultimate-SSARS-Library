# Review Report - Known Departure (AR-C 90)

## Qualified Conclusion

Use this report when:
- There is a material departure from the applicable framework
- The departure is not pervasive (if pervasive, use adverse conclusion)
- Accountant is independent of the entity (required)

---

## Report Template

```
                    INDEPENDENT ACCOUNTANT'S REVIEW REPORT


To the [Board of Directors / Shareholders / Partners / Members] of
[Company Name]
[City, State]


I (We) have reviewed the accompanying financial statements of [Company Name], which
comprise the [balance sheet / statement of financial position] as of [Month Day,
Year], and the related statements of income, [changes in stockholders' equity /
changes in partners' capital / changes in members' equity], and cash flows for the
[year / period] then ended, and the related notes to the financial statements.


Management's Responsibility for the Financial Statements

Management is responsible for the preparation and fair presentation of these
financial statements in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted
in the United States of America; this includes the design, implementation, and
maintenance of internal control relevant to the preparation and fair presentation
of financial statements that are free from material misstatement, whether due to
fraud or error.


Accountant's Responsibility

My (Our) responsibility is to conduct the review engagement in accordance with
Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services promulgated by the
Accounting and Review Services Committee of the AICPA. Those standards require
me (us) to perform procedures to obtain limited assurance as a basis for
reporting whether I am (we are) aware of any material modifications that should
be made to the financial statements for them to be in accordance with accounting
principles generally accepted in the United States of America. I (We) believe
that the results of my (our) procedures provide a reasonable basis for my (our)
conclusion.

I am (We are) required to be independent of [Company Name] and to meet my (our)
other ethical responsibilities, in accordance with the relevant ethical
requirements related to my (our) review.


Basis for Qualified Conclusion

As disclosed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company has not
[describe the departure - e.g., "capitalized lease obligations that should be
capitalized in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted in the
United States of America"]. If these [describe what should have been done - e.g.,
"lease obligations were capitalized"], [describe the effects - e.g., "property
and equipment would be increased by $XXX,XXX, long-term debt would be increased
by $XXX,XXX, and net income would be decreased by $XX,XXX"].

[OR if effects cannot be determined:]

As disclosed in Note X to the financial statements, the Company has not
[describe the departure]. The effects of this departure on the financial
statements have not been determined.


Qualified Conclusion

Based on my (our) review, except for the effects of the matter described in the
Basis for Qualified Conclusion paragraph, I am (we are) not aware of any material
modifications that should be made to the accompanying financial statements in
order for them to be in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted
in the United States of America.


[Signature of accounting firm or accountant]

[City, State]

[Date of report]
```

---

## Key Elements

### Basis for Qualified Conclusion Paragraph

This paragraph must include:
1. **Description of the departure** - What rule was violated?
2. **Reference to disclosure** (if in notes) - Where is it disclosed?
3. **Effects on financial statements** - Either:
   - Quantified effects (if determinable)
   - Statement that effects have not been determined

### Qualified Conclusion Paragraph

Must include:
- "except for the effects of the matter described in the Basis for Qualified Conclusion paragraph"
- Otherwise same as unmodified conclusion

---

## Common Departures

| Departure | Example Language |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Inventory valuation** | "The Company has stated inventory at amounts that exceed net realizable value..." |
| **Depreciation** | "The Company has not recorded depreciation on fixed assets..." |
| **Lease capitalization** | "The Company has not capitalized lease obligations that should be capitalized..." |
| **Revenue recognition** | "The Company recognizes revenue when cash is received rather than when earned..." |
| **Bad debt allowance** | "The Company has not provided an allowance for doubtful accounts..." |

---

## Effects Examples

### When Effects Are Determinable

```
If these lease obligations were capitalized in accordance with accounting
principles generally accepted in the United States of America, property and
equipment would be increased by $150,000, long-term debt would be increased
by $145,000, current maturities of long-term debt would be increased by
$25,000, and net income would be decreased by $12,000.
```

### When Effects Cannot Be Determined

```
The effects of this departure on the financial statements have not been
determined.
```

---

## When NOT to Use Qualified Conclusion

Use **Adverse Conclusion** instead when:
- The departure is pervasive to the financial statements
- Multiple significant departures exist
- The effects are so material that "except for" is not appropriate
