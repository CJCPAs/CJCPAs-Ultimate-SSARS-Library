# SSARS Glossary of Terms

## Comprehensive Definitions for Compilation and Review Services

---

## A

### Accountant
A practitioner (or the practitioner's firm) conducting a SSARS engagement. The term includes partners and staff.

### Adverse Conclusion (Review)
A conclusion that the financial statements are not in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework due to the pervasiveness of material departures.

### Agreed-Upon Terms
The terms of the engagement agreed between the accountant and the client, documented in an engagement letter.

### Analytical Procedures
Evaluations of financial information through analysis of plausible relationships among both financial and nonfinancial data. In reviews, these are required procedures.

### Applicable Financial Reporting Framework
The financial reporting framework adopted by management and, where appropriate, those charged with governance, that is acceptable in view of the nature of the entity and the objective of the financial statements.

### AR-C Section
The designation for sections in the AICPA Professional Standards covering Accounting and Review Services.

### Assembly (of Documentation)
The process of organizing the engagement file and completing the administrative process of assembling the final engagement file after the report date.

### Assurance
The accountant's satisfaction as to the reliability of an assertion being made. Limited assurance is provided in reviews; no assurance is provided in compilations or preparations.

---

## B

### Basis of Accounting
The body of accounting principles used to prepare financial statements (e.g., GAAP, cash basis, tax basis, regulatory basis).

### Bookkeeping
The recording of transactions and maintaining financial records - a service separate from compilation or review.

---

## C

### Cash Basis of Accounting
A special purpose framework in which revenues are recognized when cash is received and expenses are recognized when cash is paid.

### Compilation Engagement
An engagement to apply accounting and financial reporting expertise to assist management in the presentation of financial statements without undertaking to obtain or provide any assurance.

### Conclusion
In a review engagement, the accountant's expression of limited assurance (e.g., "not aware of any material modifications").

### Conditional Contribution
A contribution that depends on the occurrence of a specified future and uncertain event (applies to nonprofit accounting).

### Continuation Agreement
A document used in lieu of a new engagement letter when terms remain unchanged.

### Contractual Adjustments
In healthcare, the difference between gross charges and amounts expected to be received from third-party payers.

### Covered Member
Under AICPA independence rules, an individual in a position to influence an attest engagement.

---

## D

### Departure from Framework
A deviation from the applicable financial reporting framework that may require disclosure in the accountant's report.

### Direct Financial Interest
A financial interest directly owned or under the individual's control.

### Documentation
The record of procedures performed, evidence obtained, and conclusions reached. Also called workpapers or working papers.

### Documentation Completion Date
The date by which assembly of the final engagement file should be completed (generally 60 days after report date).

---

## E

### Emphasis of Matter Paragraph
An optional paragraph in the accountant's report that refers to a matter appropriately presented or disclosed in the financial statements that is of such importance that it is fundamental to users' understanding.

### Engagement Letter
The written agreement between the accountant and client specifying the terms of the engagement.

### Engagement Quality Review
A process designed to provide an objective evaluation of significant judgments and conclusions.

### Entity Understanding
Knowledge of the entity's business, operations, industry, accounting policies, and internal control sufficient to perform the engagement.

### Ethical Requirements
Requirements related to professional ethics, including independence when applicable.

### Explicit Waiver
A waiver by a user from the accountant's legal liability.

---

## F

### Fair Presentation
Financial statements that present fairly the financial position, results of operations, and cash flows in accordance with the applicable framework.

### Financial Interest
An ownership interest in an equity or debt security, or any investment or business relationship with an attest client.

### Financial Reporting Framework
The set of criteria used to determine measurement, recognition, presentation, and disclosure of material items in financial statements.

### Financial Statements
A structured representation of historical financial information, including related notes.

### Framework
See Financial Reporting Framework.

### Fraud
An intentional act by one or more individuals resulting in a misrepresentation of financial statements.

---

## G

### GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)
The standard framework of guidelines for financial accounting in a particular jurisdiction (in the US, promulgated by FASB).

### General Purpose Financial Statements
Financial statements prepared in accordance with a general purpose framework intended for a wide range of users.

### Going Concern
The assumption that an entity will continue in operation for the foreseeable future. Substantial doubt about this must be evaluated and disclosed.

### Gross Profit Margin
The percentage of revenue remaining after deducting cost of goods sold.

---

## H

### Historical Financial Information
Information expressed in financial terms regarding a particular entity, derived primarily from that entity's accounting system.

---

## I

### Immaterial
Not significant enough to affect users' decisions. Opposite of material.

### Independence
The freedom from relationships that may impair objectivity and professional skepticism. Required for reviews; not required (but must be disclosed if lacking) for compilations.

### Independence Impairment
A circumstance that causes the accountant to lack independence with respect to the client.

### Indirect Financial Interest
A financial interest beneficially owned through an investment vehicle, partnership, or trust.

### Inquiry
A procedure consisting of seeking information from knowledgeable persons within or outside the entity.

### Internal Control
A process designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives relating to operations, reporting, and compliance.

---

## J

### Joint Venture
A business arrangement in which two or more parties agree to pool resources for a specific task while remaining independent entities.

### Judgment
See Professional Judgment.

---

## K

### Known Departure
A departure from the applicable financial reporting framework that the accountant becomes aware of during the engagement.

### Known Misstatement
A misstatement that has been specifically identified by the accountant.

---

## L

### Legend
A statement required on each page of prepared financial statements indicating no assurance is provided (AR-C 70).

### Letter of Intent
A document outlining the preliminary agreement between parties before a definitive agreement is executed.

### Limited Assurance
The level of assurance obtained in a review engagement - less than reasonable assurance (audit) but more than no assurance (compilation).

### Liquidity
The ability to meet short-term obligations.

---

## M

### Management
Those with executive responsibility for the conduct of the entity's operations.

### Management Representation Letter
A written statement by management confirming certain representations made during the engagement. Required in reviews; recommended in compilations.

### Material Misstatement
A misstatement that is of sufficient magnitude to affect users' decisions.

### Materiality
The magnitude of an omission or misstatement that would influence users' decisions.

### Modified Report/Conclusion
A report that deviates from the standard form due to departures, scope limitations, or other factors.

---

## N

### Net Asset Classification
In nonprofit accounting, the categorization of net assets as either "with donor restrictions" or "without donor restrictions."

### Nonattest Service
A service that does not provide assurance, such as tax preparation, consulting, or bookkeeping.

### Noncompliance
Failure to comply with laws, regulations, or contractual requirements.

---

## O

### OCBOA (Other Comprehensive Basis of Accounting)
Now called Special Purpose Frameworks - accounting bases other than GAAP.

### Obvious Material Error
An error that is apparent to the accountant reading the financial statements.

### Opinion
The expression of the auditor's conclusion on financial statements - not used in SSARS engagements.

### Other-Matter Paragraph
A paragraph referring to a matter other than those presented in the financial statements that is relevant to users' understanding.

---

## P

### Percentage of Completion
A method of recognizing revenue on long-term contracts based on the proportion of work completed.

### Performance Materiality
A lower threshold than overall materiality, used to reduce the risk that aggregate misstatements exceed overall materiality.

### Pervasive
A term describing effects or potential effects of misstatements that are widespread in the financial statements.

### Planning
The process of developing an overall strategy and approach for the engagement.

### Predecessor Accountant
The accountant who previously reported on or was engaged to report on the client's financial statements.

### Preparation Engagement
An engagement to prepare financial statements in accordance with an applicable framework, with a legend but no report.

### Prior Period Adjustment
An adjustment to the opening balance of retained earnings for the correction of an error in prior period financial statements.

### Pro Forma Financial Information
Financial information that shows what the financial position or results would have been if a specific transaction had occurred at an earlier date.

### Professional Judgment
The application of relevant training, knowledge, and experience in making informed decisions.

### Professional Skepticism
An attitude that includes a questioning mind and critical assessment of evidence.

---

## Q

### Qualified Conclusion
In a review, a conclusion modified due to a departure from the framework or scope limitation that is material but not pervasive.

### Quality Management System
A system of policies, procedures, and practices designed to ensure quality in professional services (addressed in SSARS 26).

---

## R

### Reasonable Basis
Sufficient evidence to support a conclusion or representation.

### Recoverability
The ability to recover the carrying amount of an asset through future cash flows.

### Related Party
A party that is related to the entity through ownership, control, or other close relationships.

### Relevant Ethical Requirements
The ethical requirements to which the engagement team is subject.

### Representation Letter
See Management Representation Letter.

### Restatement
Revision of previously issued financial statements to correct errors.

### Retention Period
The length of time engagement documentation must be retained (typically minimum 5 years).

### Retainage
In construction, amounts withheld from contractor payments pending satisfactory completion.

### Revenue Recognition
The process of recording revenue when it is earned and realizable.

### Review Engagement
An engagement to obtain limited assurance through inquiry and analytical procedures.

---

## S

### Safeguards
Actions or other measures that may eliminate a threat to independence or reduce it to an acceptable level.

### Scope Limitation
A restriction on the accountant's ability to perform necessary procedures.

### Self-Review Threat
The threat that the accountant will not appropriately evaluate work previously performed.

### Significant Matter
A matter of importance that affects the engagement or financial statements.

### Special Purpose Financial Statements
Financial statements prepared in accordance with a special purpose framework.

### Special Purpose Framework
A financial reporting framework other than GAAP (e.g., cash basis, tax basis, regulatory basis, contractual basis).

### SSARS (Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services)
The authoritative standards for compilation and review engagements, issued by ARSC.

### Subsequent Events
Events occurring between the financial statement date and the report date.

### Substantial Doubt
The level of concern about going concern that triggers disclosure requirements.

### Substantively All Disclosures
The term used when most but not all required disclosures are present (typically "substantially all" means nearly complete).

### Successor Accountant
An accountant who is being considered or has been engaged to perform a current engagement.

---

## T

### Tax Basis of Accounting
A special purpose framework based on income tax regulations.

### Threats and Safeguards Approach
The approach to evaluating independence by identifying threats and applying safeguards.

### Those Charged with Governance
The person(s) or organization(s) with responsibility for overseeing the entity's strategic direction and accountability.

### Trend Analysis
Analytical procedure comparing current period amounts to prior periods.

---

## U

### Undue Influence
Threats or pressure that affects the accountant's objectivity.

### Unmodified Conclusion
A review conclusion that is not modified by any departures or limitations.

### Unrestricted Net Assets
In nonprofit accounting, now called "net assets without donor restrictions."

---

## V

### Variable Consideration
In revenue recognition, consideration that can vary based on future events.

### Variance Analysis
Analytical procedure comparing actual results to expectations or budgets.

---

## W

### Withdrawal
The accountant's discontinuation of an engagement before completion.

### With Donor Restrictions
In nonprofit accounting, net assets subject to donor-imposed restrictions.

### Without Donor Restrictions
In nonprofit accounting, net assets not subject to donor-imposed restrictions.

### Working Capital
Current assets minus current liabilities.

### Workpapers
See Documentation.

### Write-Up Services
See Bookkeeping.
