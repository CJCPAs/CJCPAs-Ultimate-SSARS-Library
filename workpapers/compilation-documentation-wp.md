## Example Entries

### Example: Understanding of Entity (Section D)

**GOOD DOCUMENTATION:**
```
Entity: ABC Plumbing, LLC
Industry: Residential plumbing services
Structure: Single-member LLC, tax basis reporting
Employees: 5 (owner plus 4 plumbers)
Accounting: QuickBooks Online, maintained by owner's spouse
Revenue recognition: Cash basis - recorded when payment received
Significant policies: No inventory (supplies expensed when purchased),
vehicles depreciated straight-line over 5 years

Understanding is sufficient to compile financial statements.
```

**POOR DOCUMENTATION:**
```
Small plumbing company. Cash basis.
```

### Example: Reading Procedures (Section E.2)

**GOOD DOCUMENTATION:**
```
Read financial statements on 2/15/XX. Noted the following:

1. Accounts receivable of $45,000 - Unusual for cash basis entity.
   INQUIRY: Per owner, these are credit card payments in transit
   at year-end. Determined appropriate for cash basis as
   economically received. No adjustment needed.

2. Vehicle loan shows $0 current portion.
   INQUIRY: Loan is interest-only with balloon in 20X5.
   Reclassified $35,000 to long-term. CORRECTED.

3. No obvious material errors noted after corrections above.
   Financial statements appear appropriate in form.
```

**POOR DOCUMENTATION:**
```
Read financial statements. No issues.
```
